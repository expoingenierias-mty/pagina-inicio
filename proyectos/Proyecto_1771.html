Assessing Urban Accessibility: A Comparable Study to Main Destinations in the Distrito Tec, Monterrey, Mexico and the Stanford District, Bay Area, USA

To generate sustainable cities, focused on combating climate change, it is necessary to evaluate urban accessibility. This project analyzes the case of Distrito Tec, Monterrey, Mexico compared to Distrito Stanford, San Francisco Bay Area, United States, to determine the time it takes for a person to leave his or her home and reach main destinations, such as supermarkets and schools. And discuss the importance of having updated and robust databases for urban analysis and urban improvement proposals.
