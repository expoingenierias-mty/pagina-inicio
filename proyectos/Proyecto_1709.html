Hyper-heuristic Model Based on Neural Networks for Tailoring Metaheuristics in Continuous Optimization Problems

Metaheuristics (MHs) have been proven to be powerful algorithms for solving non-linear optimization problems over discrete, continuous, or mixed domains, with applications ranging from basic sciences to applied technologies. Nowadays, the literature is prolific with MHs based on outstanding ideas, but often, they are just recombining elements from other methods. To avoid the frenetic tendency of proposing methods, a standard model has been proposed that customizes population-based MHs, which uses simple heuristics extracted from well-known MHs. The framework corresponding to this model can be found as Customising Optimising Metaheuristic via Hyper-heuristic Search (CUSTOMHyS), which includes the implementation of models that explores a heuristic space. Still, they are limited by the nature of the metaheuristic used in such models. A field of action and improvement that has not been explored is to implement a model that takes advantage of previous results and learns from them to boost the performance of the tailoring process. For that reason, we propose a hyper-heuristic model based on Neural Networks that is trained with processed sequences of heuristics to identify patterns that can be used in the generation of modified metaheuristics.
