Greenhouse gas emissions reduction from fuel switching in HDVs and its application in the international carbon market

The fuel consumption baseline for heavy-duty vehicles in Mexico was determined, based on measurements under real driving operation given by eleven freight transport companies. With this information greenhouse gas emissions of HDVs were calculated, demonstrating that a switch from diesel to natural gas fuel can contribute to a reduction from 10 to 20% in GHG emissions. The aim of this project is to apply these findings into the voluntary carbon market so that these GHG reductions could result in profits for companies.
