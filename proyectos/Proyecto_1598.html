Mathematical Modeling and Implementation of a Digital Twin as Technology to support Discrete Event Control Teaching

This work depicts the integration of emerging technologies for simulating multiple systems such as industrial cameras, programmable logic controllers, robots with ROS (Robot Operating System) and the cloud into a simulated world environment. The proposed method shows a solution to test the performance of a factory based on the interaction and data provided by systems such as Datalogic cameras for industrial vision, CODESYS for plc programming, process simulate to create virtual environments and ROS robots. The expected result is the development of a highly efficient industrial simulation environment capable of managing real-world problems with robust mathematical modeling. This research contributes to the development of a cost-effective way to teach industrial skills inside universities or industries.
